# Random Software — Brand Guide

## Who We Are

Random Software is a one-person studio run by Alex Wittenberg (CEO of airCFO). It's a portfolio of intentional software projects built with AI — tools that exist because they should, not because they need to produce venture-scale returns.

The name "Random Software" is a deliberate wink. The projects aren't random at all — they're opinionated, specific, and personal. But the range is unpredictable, and the vibe is "I built this because I wanted it to exist."

## Brand Personality

**Confident but not corporate.** This isn't a startup trying to raise a Series A. There's no mission statement committee. The voice is direct, self-aware, and occasionally funny — like talking to a smart friend who builds things on the side.

**Intentional but not precious.** Every project has a clear point of view, but nothing is overproduced. The aesthetic says "I care about quality" without saying "I spent six months on the font."

**Technical but accessible.** The audience includes builders, founders, and curious people — not just engineers. Avoid jargon. Explain through story, not architecture diagrams.

**Punk rock energy, gallery presentation.** The manifesto explicitly draws a line from vibecoding to punk rock — democratizing creation, rejecting gatekeepers. But the site presents the work like a curated gallery, not a cluttered garage. The tension between DIY spirit and polished presentation is the brand.

## Voice & Tone

- First person, conversational ("I built this because...")
- Self-deprecating where appropriate ("a one-person team with ADHD and a Claude Max subscription")
- Opinionated — takes positions, names specific trade-offs
- No marketing speak, no buzzwords, no "leveraging synergies"
- Short sentences. Direct. Says what it means.
- Comfortable referencing real things: SXSW, Rick Rubin, Claude Code, Rainey Street

## Visual Identity

### Colors

| Token | Hex | Role |
|-------|-----|------|
| Brand Green | `#00B140` | Primary — CTAs, links, active states, hover accents |
| Brand Coral | `#FF6B5A` | Accent — underlines, highlighted borders, secondary emphasis |
| Base Background | `#FAFAFA` | Page background — light, airy, gallery-like |
| Base Text | `#2C2C2C` | Primary text — warm dark, not harsh black |
| Base Border | `#E5E5E5` | Dividers, card borders — subtle, recedes |

The green is confident and energetic without being corporate. The coral adds warmth and personality — it shows up in moments of emphasis (hover underlines, highlighted design principles). Together they feel modern and alive without being loud.

The light background with warm dark text creates a gallery aesthetic — clean, breathable, focused on the content.

### Typography

**Space Grotesk** — a geometric sans-serif with just enough character to feel intentional without being quirky. It reads as modern and technical but approachable. Used everywhere (headings and body).

| Level | Size (desktop) | Weight | Notes |
|-------|---------------|--------|-------|
| Hero | 64px (4rem) | Bold | Homepage headline only. Scales to 30px on mobile. |
| Display | 48px (3rem) | Bold | Project titles, page headings. Scales to 24px on mobile. |
| H1 | 32px (2rem) | Bold | Section headings in content pages |
| H2 | 24px (1.5rem) | Semibold | Subsection headings |
| Body | 16px (1rem) | Regular | Paragraph text, descriptions |
| Small | 14px (0.875rem) | Regular | Footer, metadata, labels |

### Layout Principles

- **Generous whitespace** — the site breathes. Large vertical spacing between sections (120px desktop, 64px mobile). Content never feels cramped.
- **Single column, centered** — max-width containers (1152px for homepage, 768px for content pages). No sidebars, no grids-for-the-sake-of-grids.
- **Cards as containers** — projects and heroes are presented in bordered cards with subtle shadows. The border + shadow creates depth without heaviness.
- **Animated shine border** — the homepage hero uses a gradient border animation (green → coral) that slowly cycles. It's the one moment of visual flourish — everything else is restrained.

### Interaction Design

- Hover states use brand green (text and backgrounds)
- Link underlines animate from left to right using brand coral
- All transitions are 200ms ease-in-out — quick enough to feel responsive, slow enough to notice
- Respects `prefers-reduced-motion`

## Logo Direction

The logo should feel like it belongs in this world:

**What it should convey:**
- A solo builder's mark — personal, not institutional
- Creative confidence — "I know what I'm making and why"
- The tension between "random" (playful, unpredictable) and "software" (precise, functional)
- Could sit comfortably on both a clean portfolio site and a sticker on a laptop

**What it should NOT be:**
- Corporate or startup-y (no abstract swooshes, no gradient orbs)
- Overly minimalist to the point of being forgettable
- Literal (no dice, no code brackets, no circuit boards)
- Childish — playful yes, but not juvenile

**Aesthetic references:**
- The restraint of a gallery logo with the energy of an indie record label
- Space Grotesk as the typeface (or something in the same geometric sans family)
- Should work in single-color (green on white, white on dark) as well as the full green + coral palette
- Needs to be legible at small sizes (nav bar, favicon) and look good at large sizes (hero, social cards)

**Possible directions to explore:**
- A wordmark with one unexpected element — a letter that breaks the grid, a subtle rotation, a color split
- A monogram or symbol that works as a standalone favicon alongside a full wordmark
- Something that plays with the word "random" — controlled disorder, a pattern that's almost regular but not quite
- Typography-forward — let the letterforms do the work rather than relying on an icon
