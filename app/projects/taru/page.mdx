## Origin Story

The name "Taru" (樽) means "barrel" in Japanese—a vessel that holds and transforms over time. Just as a barrel ages whiskey into something refined, Taru processes raw information streams into distilled knowledge.

I built Taru because I was drowning in information. Every morning, I'd open 20+ browser tabs of RSS feeds, bookmarked articles, and newsletter digests. I'd skim headlines, save interesting pieces to read later, and promptly forget about them. My "read later" list became a graveyard of good intentions.

The problem wasn't *access* to information—it was *processing* it. I needed something that didn't just aggregate content, but understood it. Something that could extract the signal from the noise, remember context across sources, and build a knowledge graph that grew smarter over time.

So I built Taru. Not as a passive inbox, but as an active processing system. It reads everything, remembers everything, and helps me make connections I would have missed.

## What It Does

### RSS Digest Automation

Configure your feeds once. Every morning, receive a curated digest with AI-generated summaries, key insights, relevance scoring, and one-click saves to your knowledge base.

Taru doesn't just collect articles—it reads them, extracts what matters, and presents the information in a format optimized for quick decision-making: read now, save for later, or skip entirely.

### Podcast Production Pipeline

Transform written content into audio formats automatically. Taru handles the entire pipeline:

1. **Content ingestion** from blogs, newsletters, and RSS feeds
2. **Script generation** optimized for audio delivery with natural pacing
3. **Voice synthesis** with multiple voice options and emotional tone
4. **Episode management** with metadata, show notes, and RSS feed generation

Perfect for consuming long-form content during commutes or workouts.

### Memory Extraction Engine

This is where Taru shines. It builds a persistent memory layer across all your content:

- **Entity recognition**: Automatically tracks people, companies, and concepts mentioned across sources
- **Topic clustering**: Groups related content and reveals themes you didn't know existed
- **Temporal tracking**: Shows how ideas evolve over time in your reading
- **Cross-referencing**: Connects disparate sources that mention related concepts

The more you use Taru, the smarter it gets. It learns what matters to you and surfaces connections that would take hours to find manually.

### Built for Creators

Taru isn't just for consumption—it's a creation tool. When you're writing, researching, or building, Taru can:

- Surface relevant past reading on demand
- Generate summaries of topic clusters for quick reference
- Identify gaps in your knowledge graph
- Suggest connections between projects and concepts

It's like having a research assistant who has read everything you've ever saved and can recall it instantly.
